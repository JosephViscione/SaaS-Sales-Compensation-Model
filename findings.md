# Veltora Inc. — FY 2025 Compensation Analysis
## Purpose: CFO Review — 2026 Planning Cycle

The following findings are based on a fictional dataset designed 
to reflect realistic B2B SaaS dynamics. FY 2025 was a year of 
two halves. The comp structure worked exactly as designed in H1, 
then ran into headwinds in H2 that exposed a few things worth 
addressing before the 2026 plan locks.

## Q1 Accelerators Were Expensive and Earned

The org hit roughly 118% attainment in Q1. Multiple reps triggered 
Tier 1 and Tier 2 accelerators across Enterprise and Mid-Market. 
The extra comp cost was real, but so was the revenue. The 
accelerator structure did exactly what it was designed to do. 
No changes recommended here.

## H2 Softening Is a Pattern, Not Bad Luck

August dropped 15 to 20% across all three segments simultaneously. 
That is not a coincidence. It is a seasonal pattern baked into 
enterprise buying cycles, and it will happen again in 2026. 
Veltora should either build a seasonality adjustment into Q3 
quotas or front-load pipeline targets in Q2 to create a buffer 
before the slowdown hits.

## Rep Churn Costs More Than the Salary Savings

Three reps left mid-year. On paper, that looks like payroll relief. 
In practice, it meant months of uncovered territory, ramping 
replacements operating at 40% and 70% of quota, and a pipeline 
that never got built. The real cost of attrition is not the 
salary — it is the quota gap. Retention should be a line item 
in the 2026 planning conversation.

## Comp Concentration Needs a Closer Look

The top 20% of earners took a disproportionate share of variable 
comp. That can mean two things: those reps are genuinely 
exceptional, or some quotas are set too low. Given the Q1 
blowout and the number of accelerator events, it is likely 
a mix of both. Quota calibration by segment should be on the 
agenda before 2026 plans are finalized.

## SMB Needs Attention

One AE departure and a mid-year PIP left the SMB segment 
shorthanded at the worst time. The replacement joined in July 
and ramped through H2, but the segment never fully recovered. 
SMB quotas and territory coverage should be reviewed before 
adding headcount — more reps in an undertooled segment does 
not solve the underlying problem.
